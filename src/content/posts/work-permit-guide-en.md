Just graduated and want to stay in Taiwan to work?

Congratulations on that decision. But before you start blasting out resumes, there is one thing you must understand first: the **work permit**.

Many overseas and international graduates do not know about this until they get an offer, then scramble at the last minute. A work permit is not just a piece of paper. It is the legal basis for working in Taiwan. Without it, even if a company hires you, you cannot start work.

## What is a work permit?

Under Taiwan's Employment Service Act, foreigners must hold a work permit to legally engage in any work in Taiwan.

Only after you obtain a work permit can you use it to apply for a residence visa and complete the full process.

In general, each work permit is issued for up to **3 years**. You can apply for extension before it expires, with no limit on the number of extensions. If you qualify as a "foreign special professional," each issuance can last up to **5 years**.

---

## Application process: 4 steps to get it done

It may sound complicated, but broken down it is really just four steps:

### ✅ Step 1: Find an employer willing to hire you

You do not apply for the work permit yourself. Your **employer applies to the Ministry of Labor on your behalf**.

So the first step is really finding a job, and finding a company that clearly says it will help you apply for a work permit. That is why "foreigner-friendly employers" matter so much. Not every company has the willingness or experience to do this.

### ✅ Step 2: The employer submits the application online

After confirming your hire, the employer must apply online through the Ministry of Labor's "Foreign Professional Work Permit Application System." Paper mailing is not accepted (except in special cases such as natural disasters or system outages).

You do not need to run this part yourself, but you should proactively ask the employer about progress instead of passively waiting for updates.

### ✅ Step 3: Wait for the review result

After submission, if the documents are complete and both the employer and you meet the requirements, review takes **7 working days from the day after receipt**.

Tip: Start the application at least three weeks before your expected start date so you have a buffer. Otherwise you may hit your start date with no permit yet, which is awkward for everyone.

### ✅ Step 4: After approval, update your residence certificate

Once the work permit is approved, take it to the National Immigration Agency to update your Alien Residence Certificate (ARC) and formally add the work purpose. Only then is the process complete and your work status truly legal.

## What documents are required?

When applying, employers typically need to prepare:

- Business certificate + applicant's Citizen Digital Certificate
- Original review fee receipt
- Application form
- Roster of foreign employees to be hired
- Copy of your passport or ARC
- Copy of the employment contract (must include employer name, employee name, job title, period, and salary)
- Copy of the company responsible person's ID card or passport
- Copy of the company's registration certificate

💡 If documents are in a foreign language, you can translate only the key parts (title, salary, period, etc.). Full translation is not required, and the applicant may translate them without extra authentication.

## Both the employer and you must qualify

A work permit is not automatically approved just because you apply. Both the employer and you must meet the rules.

### Employer requirements

| Company age | Must meet one of the following |
|---|---|
| Less than 1 year | Paid-in capital of NT$5 million or more |
| 1 year or more | Average revenue of NT$10 million or more in the most recent year or over the past 3 years |

### Foreign worker requirements

You only need to meet one of the following:

- Hold a **master's degree or higher** from a domestic or overseas university
- Hold a **bachelor's degree** plus at least 2 years of relevant work experience
- Obtain a **professional or technical personnel** certificate or practice qualification through examination
- Have 5 or more years of relevant experience with original ideas and outstanding performance
- Have worked for a multinational enterprise for 1 year or more and been assigned to Taiwan

## Overseas and international graduates have a dedicated "point system"

If you graduated from a school in Taiwan, there is good news: the Ministry of Labor has a "**new point system**" for overseas and international graduates who studied in Taiwan. It does not look at salary alone. Instead, it scores eight dimensions:

Education, salary level, special expertise, language ability, growth experience, alignment with government industrial development policy, and more. **Accumulate 70 points or more** and you meet the eligibility threshold.

This system is designed to encourage overseas and international graduates who already studied in Taiwan and understand local culture to stay and work. That is an advantage for you.

## ⚠️ Do not get these 3 things wrong

### 👉 You cannot start work before the permit is approved

This is the most common misunderstanding. Getting an offer and signing a contract does not mean you can start working.

By law, you must wait until the **work permit is approved** before officially starting. Working before that can result in fines for both you and the employer. The risk is real.

### 👉 When your ARC is about to expire, update the work permit too

A work permit's validity is usually tied to your residence certificate. If your ARC is about to expire, check your work permit status at the same time. Do not fall into the gray zone where "residence is still valid, but the work permit has already expired."

### 👉 Changing jobs means reapplying!

A work permit is issued for a **specific employer**. You cannot reuse an old permit when you switch companies. The new employer must apply for you again. Before changing jobs, confirm early that the new company is willing to handle the process.

## FAQ

**Q: What types of work can apply for a work permit?**

There are currently 7 categories open for foreign nationals: specialized or technical work, foreign managerial positions, school teachers, foreign language teachers at cram schools, sports coaches and athletes, arts and entertainment work, and contract performance personnel.

**Q: When is a work permit not required?**

The following cases do not require a separate work permit by law:
- Married to a Taiwanese national and already approved for residence
- Permanent residents
- Hired by a government agency as an advisor or researcher
- Short-term work of 30 days or less during a stay (subject to specific conditions)

**Q: Can foreigners apply for a work permit themselves?**

In most cases, the employer must apply. However, the following may apply directly to the Ministry of Labor: approved refugees with residence, permanent residents, and those living with a Taiwanese parent. You can also apply directly for an Employment Gold Card, which is jointly reviewed by the Ministry of Labor.
