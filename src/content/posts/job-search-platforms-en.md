Just arrived in Taiwan and ready to job hunt, but have no idea which platform to start with?

You are not alone. Taiwan’s job market works differently from what you may be used to. It is not only about language. Platform features, job types, and how you apply all come with their own quirks.

![Browsing job listings on a laptop in a café](https://images.pexels.com/photos/3183153/pexels-photo-3183153.jpeg?auto=compress&cs=tinysrgb&w=1600)

This article covers the four platforms overseas students use most often, what each is best at, and when to use them. No more guessing. Start where it actually helps.

---

## 1. WPORT Career Hub

**Best for: overseas students new to Taiwan’s job market**

WPORT Career Hub is built for international talent. You can apply to jobs, and you also get job-search guides and resume tools made for overseas students. In short, it is a place that understands what you are worrying about.

If you just arrived and are still figuring things out, start here because:

- Job-search guidance is written for overseas students, so you do not have to learn everything the hard way
- Resume tools help you present your experience in a format Taiwan employers understand
- Content is written from an international talent perspective, not generic advice for local applicants

💡 **Why recommend WPORT first?**

For overseas students entering Taiwan’s workplace, WPORT is a stronger starting point for three reasons:

- **Lower information barriers**: Taiwan’s workplace has many unspoken rules, from resume format and interview culture to salary negotiation. WPORT helps you learn them early.
- **Build job-search confidence**: Starting on a platform that “gets you” makes it easier to find direction instead of feeling lost.
- **A dedicated overseas-student lens**: Content targets the real challenges international talent faces, not one-size-fits-all advice.

---

## 2. 104 Job Bank

**Best for: people who want to apply widely and compare many options**

104 is Taiwan’s largest job platform. Almost every industry posts openings there, and the volume is huge.

For overseas students, 104’s biggest advantage is scale:

- Strong search filters by industry, role, salary, and location
- Easy side-by-side comparison of company offers and salary ranges
- Some listings specifically welcome international talent or list language requirements

We suggest clarifying your direction on WPORT first, then using 104 to gather openings at scale. That is far more efficient than applying at random.

---

## 3. 1111 Job Bank

**Best for: people who want to understand company culture before applying**

Beyond job listings, 1111 has a practical feature: employee reviews, salary insights, and descriptions of the work environment.

That is especially useful for overseas students:

- You can “peek” at company culture before applying and gauge how friendly it is to foreign employees
- First-hand comments from job seekers feel more real than polished company websites
- You can filter out companies that look good on the surface but are not a fit for you

If company culture and work environment matter to you, 1111 is a solid supporting tool.

---

## 4. Cai DuoDuo

**Best for: people seeking foreigner-friendly companies or cross-border roles**

Cai DuoDuo is different. It focuses on matching international talent, and most openings clearly welcome foreign applicants.

Key strengths:

- Listings are mostly from companies open to international talent, so you are less likely to apply only to learn foreigners are not hired
- Some cross-border roles or jobs where English is the main working language
- Matching is designed around foreign applicants’ needs more closely than general platforms

If your edge is multilingual ability or a cross-cultural background, Cai DuoDuo is where that advantage shows up best.

---

## 6. You do not need to rely on only one platform

People often ask: “Which one is the best?”

Honestly, there is no single answer. The most effective approach is to **use multiple platforms together**:

1. Clarify your direction and polish your resume on WPORT
2. Browse widely on 104 or 1111 and learn salary ranges
3. If you specifically want foreigner-friendly employers, also check Cai DuoDuo

Looking around and comparing more often leads you to roles that truly fit.

---

## Closing

Platforms are tools. What matters most is how clearly you know yourself.

For overseas students job hunting in Taiwan, the challenge is not only language. It is helping Taiwan employers quickly see your value. Before you mass-apply, spend time clarifying your target role and core strengths, then use these platforms with a plan. The results will look very different.
